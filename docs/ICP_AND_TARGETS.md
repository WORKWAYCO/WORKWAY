# Ideal Customer Profile & Target List

> **Goal**: Acquire first 10 paying customers by defining who needs WORKWAY most.

## Ideal Customer Profile (ICP)

### Primary ICP: Integration Agencies

**Profile**:
- **Company Size**: 5-50 employees
- **Services**: Custom integrations, workflow automation, SaaS implementation consulting
- **Current Tools**: Zapier, Make, custom Node.js scripts
- **Pain Point**: Building the same workflows repeatedly for different clients
- **Budget**: $500-$2K/month for platforms that reduce client delivery time
- **Decision Maker**: Technical founder, CTO, VP Engineering

**Why They Buy**:
- Stop rebuilding the same workflows for every client
- Monetize internal tools by publishing to marketplace
- Private workflows let them deliver enterprise value faster
- Platform handles OAuth, hosting, scaling (they focus on business logic)

**Value Proposition**: Turn your client work into recurring revenue. Build once, sell many times.

### Secondary ICP: Technical Solopreneurs

**Profile**:
- **Role**: Independent developer, consultant, "technical founder"
- **Current State**: Building one-off automations for clients
- **Pain Point**: Trading time for money, no leverage
- **Budget**: $0-$500/month (highly price-sensitive)
- **Decision Maker**: Self

**Why They Buy**:
- Escape hourly billing by publishing workflows to marketplace
- Keep 100% of upfront revenue, WORKWAY only charges per-execution
- TypeScript feels native (vs. visual builders)

**Value Proposition**: Build workflows once, earn while you sleep.

### Tertiary ICP: Enterprise DevOps Teams

**Profile**:
- **Company Size**: 500+ employees
- **Department**: Platform Engineering, DevOps, Internal Tools
- **Pain Point**: SaaS sprawl, integration debt, tribal knowledge in Zapier accounts
- **Budget**: $5K-$50K/year for workflow infrastructure
- **Decision Maker**: VP Engineering, Director of Platform

**Why They Buy**:
- Code-based workflows fit existing DevOps culture (git, CI/CD, code review)
- Self-hosting option (important for regulated industries)
- Escape per-task pricing (Zapier bills explode at enterprise scale)

**Value Proposition**: Bring your integrations into your codebase. Own your automation.

## Outreach Strategy

### Phase 1: Warm Leads (Week 1)

- **Who**: Waitlist signups, npm package users, GitHub stars
- **Message**: "You signed up for WORKWAY. We're live. Here's early access."
- **Goal**: 3 paying customers from warm leads

### Phase 2: Agency Outreach (Weeks 2-3)

- **Who**: Zapier/Make partners, Notion consultants
- **Message**: "Turn your client workflows into marketplace products."
- **Offer**: First 3 months free (prove ROI first)
- **Goal**: 5 agencies building private workflows

### Phase 3: Community Launch (Week 4)

- **Where**: Hacker News, Indie Hackers, Dev.to, r/SideProject
- **Message**: "TypeScript workflows that pay you per execution."
- **Goal**: 100+ developers trying the platform, 2 paying customers

### Phase 4: Enterprise Pilots (Ongoing)

- **Who**: F500 platform teams (via LinkedIn outreach)
- **Message**: "Own your integrations. Self-hosting available."
- **Offer**: Pilot program with dedicated support
- **Goal**: 1-2 enterprise contracts ($5K-$50K ACV)
